**To the Editors of Nature,**

We wish to submit our manuscript entitled **"A Cross-Domain Pressure Synchronization Index Reveals Supercritical Phase Transitions in Complex Social-Financial Systems"** for consideration as a **Nature Letter**.

**Why Nature?**  
This work identifies a **previously uncharacterized class of phase transition** in human social-financial systems. Using a unified Pressure Synchronization Index (UPSI) across six independent domains—Chinese history (-500 to 1900 AD), Mesopotamia (-3200 BCE), ancient Rome (-509 to 476 AD), Chinese finance (2018–2026), global finance (1927–2026), and global politics (-218 to 2022)—we demonstrate that crises are not sequential "contagions" but **simultaneously emergent synchronization events**. The spectral signature (Hurst H = 0.958, power-law exponent β = 1.66) identifies the regime as **supercritical**—more extreme than the classical Ising critical point (β ≈ 1)—and represents, to our knowledge, the first observation of a supercritical phase transition in complex social systems spanning 4,200 years and ~3 million observations.

**Paradigm-Shifting Contributions**  
We believe this manuscript meets Nature's criteria for "exceptional scientific importance" for four reasons:

1. **Cross-domain unification**: A single three-dimensional index (Material degradation × Social fragmentation × Elite disengagement) explains crisis synchronization across civilizations, financial markets, and political regimes—domains previously studied in isolation.

2. **Counterintuitive empirical discoveries**: Our lead-lag analysis overturns three prevailing assumptions: (i) VIX is a genuine **leading indicator** (17-day lead over equity, p < 0.001), not a realized-volatility proxy; (ii) Gold is a **crisis follower** (1-day lag), not a hedge; (iii) Global market crises are **synchronous** (lag-0 correlation r > 0.5 trans-Atlantic), rejecting the sequential contagion model.

3. **Physical theory grounding**: The supercritical signature (β = 1.66 > 1.0) places human social systems in a new universality class—closer to a melting transition than a continuous phase transition—bridging statistical physics, econophysics, and historical sociology.

4. **Causal identification and predictive validation**: Newey-West HAC correction and Propensity Score Matching confirm robustness; a future-blind test (training on 2020–2023 PSI) correctly anticipated the 2024 Snowball crash.

The manuscript is 4 pages, 3 figures, ~1,200 words main text plus Extended Data—concise yet dense with cross-disciplinary impact. We have prepared comprehensive Supplementary Information (12 sections, 15 references) detailing data sources (CBDB, CDLI, Wikidata, yfinance, FRED, OWID), Bayesian model specification (PyMC, r̂ = 1.000), spectral methodology, and causal identification protocols.
