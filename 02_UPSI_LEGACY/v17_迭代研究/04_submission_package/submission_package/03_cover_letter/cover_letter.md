Dear Editor,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript, "A Cross-Domain Pressure Synchronization Index for Crisis Detection Across Civilizations," for consideration as a **Letter** in *Nature*.

**Why Nature?** This work introduces a unified quantitative framework—the Unified Pressure Synchronization Index (UPSI)—that detects crises across financial markets, political regimes, and ancient civilizations using a single three-dimensional signature (Material-Fragmentation-Disengagement). Validated across **eight independent domains** totaling ~3.6 million observations over 5,500 years, with recall ≥75% in six domains and 100% in four, this represents a rare empirical bridge between complexity science, historical dynamics, and financial econometrics.

**What is new?** Three advances distinguish this work from existing crisis-detection literature:

1. **Cross-domain unification.** While financial early-warning indicators (BIS, IMF, FSB), structural-demographic theory (Turchin, Goldstone), and complexity-based systemic risk (Battiston, Scheffer) have each advanced understanding within single domains, no prior work has demonstrated that the *same* three-dimensional index structure captures crises across financial, political, and historical systems with no domain-specific parameter tuning.

2. **Mathematical duality: PSI + SPI.** We introduce the Pressure Synchronization Index (PSI, level-based, low-pass filter) and its mathematically dual companion, the Sudden Pressure Indicator (SPI, velocity-based, high-pass filter). Together they form a four-quadrant state-space classifier (Stable, Gradual Decline, Sudden Crisis, Accelerating Collapse) that captures both slow-burn and burst-onset crises—a capability no single-indicator framework possesses.

3. **Cross-methodological validation.** We validate UPSI not only on Chinese biographies (CBDB), cuneiform archives (CDLI/ORACC), and financial markets (yfinance), but also on the **Seshat Global History Databank**—expert-coded structural variables (population, territory, hierarchy levels) from 11 Natural Geographic Areas across Africa, Europe, Asia, and the Americas. The same 0.4/0.3/0.3 weights and -0.5 threshold, derived from Chinese history and finance, achieve 75% recall on entirely independent data types with zero parameter tuning. This demonstrates that the crisis signature is not an artifact of any single proxy.

**Unexpected findings** challenge conventional wisdom: VIX leads equity by 17 days (not follows); gold lags equity by 1 day (fails as safe haven); global PSI synchronizes without Granger causality (rejecting sequential-contagion models); and European markets (DE/FR/UK) are systemic-risk epicenters surpassing the US (PageRank analysis).

**Honest limitations** are prominently reported: small historical samples (n=5–7 dynasties), elite bias in CBDB, near-random baseline ROC AUC (0.48–0.59), low Seshat precision (5.8%) due to coarse 100-year timestep, and the exploratory nature of cross-civilization comparisons. We explicitly frame UPSI as a **synchronizer, not a predictor**—a monitoring tool, not a forecasting oracle.

**Policy relevance** is immediate: a cloud-deployable, zero-cost Dashboard (GitHub Actions + gh-pages, MIT license) is operational and ready for central-bank or regulator adoption. This addresses a key barrier in emerging markets that lack Bloomberg terminals.
